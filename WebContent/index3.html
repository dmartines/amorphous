In 2013, global ad spending reached $500 billion.

Each of these circles represents $500M in media.

If you are one of the world's largest brands, you can buy one or maybe a couple of the circles.

Even if you can afford a circle, its value is declining.

TV advertising is about 2/3 as effective as it was 10 years ago.

Digital ads are even worse, being only 1/4 as effective over the same period.

If you want to make something 10 percent better, you just need to be 10 percent better at what you're currently doing. Work harder, longer, faster. But if you want to make something 10 times better, you need to start over.

We've built a company that is able to continuously start over by having no predetermined form.

Having no form allows the problem and the opportunity to define the solution, not the other way around. It encourages openness and collaboration with partners, thought leaders and customers. It creates the conditions for a rapid and iterative development process, with an emphasis on prototyping, learning, and improving.

The result? Ideas that outperform traditional advertising. Like this.
